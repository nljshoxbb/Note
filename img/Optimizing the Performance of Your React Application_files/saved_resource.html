        Great article! I have 2 points
        1) I would plug "React Reconciliation" - Developers who are consistently having performance hits don't understand this process and should give it a serious read. react-addons-perf isn't meant to be an excuse to not understand how Reacts Reconciliation works but rather a final way to confirm to ones self their assumptions about performance, how the reconciliation process is taking place, and how to correct or optimize.
        https://facebook.github.io/...

        2) It's dangerous to constantly use PureComponent instead of shouldComponentUpdate inside a standard React.Component. Here's why (according to the React documentation)

        "...Furthermore, React.PureComponent's shouldComponentUpdate() skips prop updates for the whole component subtree. Make sure all the children components are also "pure". " -https://facebook.github.io/...
